Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between UI and UX design?

UI (User Interface) design is focused on the visual elements of a product—the look and feel, colors, typography, and button placement. UX (User Experience) design is concerned with how a user interacts with the product—the overall journey, ease of use, and efficiency of the flow. In short, UI is the "looks," and UX is the "feels."

Can you redesign an existing product?

Yes, we specialize in product redesigns. We start by conducting a comprehensive UX audit to identify pain points and opportunities for improvement. Our redesign process focuses on enhancing usability, modernizing the interface, and improving key performance indicators.

How do you ensure the design is ready for development?

We provide a complete handoff package that includes high-fidelity prototypes, interactive wireframes, a full design system (components, typography, colors), and detailed documentation. Our designers work closely with your development team to ensure a seamless implementation.

What tools do you use for design?

We use industry-standard tools to create our designs. Our primary tools include Figma for collaborative design and prototyping, Sketch for UI design, and Adobe XD. For user testing and analytics, we use tools like UserTesting and Hotjar.
